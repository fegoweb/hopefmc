MEDICATION POLICY

Narcotic Pain Medications:
We believe that narcotics are good for a limited number of short term medical problems however we do not prescribe narcotics for long term use. We DO NOT prescribe medication(s) for chronic pain conditions, however we will gladly refer you to a pain specialist or pain clinic for your chronic pain needs. If you are taking prescription pain medications or other controlled substances prescribed by another medical provider you will need to contact that provider for your prescription refills.

Prescription Refills:
If you are a patient of HOPE Family Medical Center we will be happy to refill prescriptions that we prescribe. New patients will need to transfer their medical records to the HOPE Family Medical Center to establish continuity of care before we can refill prescriptions prescribed by other medical providers. we can refill prescriptions prescribed by other providers.

Controlled Substance Refills:
New patients will need to transfer their medical records to the HOPE Family Medical Center before we can consider refilling controlled prescriptions that are prescribed by other medical providers. We will not refill controlled substance prescriptions at you first clinic appointment. We will also order a controlled substance (DOPL) report for all patients who are on a controlled substance medication prescribed by another provider and/or if you request a prescription for a controlled substance medication.
